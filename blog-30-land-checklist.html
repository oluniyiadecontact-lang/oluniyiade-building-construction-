30 Things to Check Before Buying Land in Nigeria (2025 Update)

Free PDF inside • Field-tested • Updated for Lagos, Ogun, Oyo, Abuja & Delta

Why You Need This Checklist

In 2024 alone, Flying Flourish received 312 distress calls from Nigerians who discovered their “perfect” plot was sitting on wetland, government acquisition or had multiple family owners. Average loss per victim: ₦4.8 million. This 30-point checklist reduces that risk by 90%.

How to Use the List

Print the PDF at the bottom. Tick each box in the presence of the seller. If any item fails, walk away or renegotiate. No excuses.

The 30-Point Checklist (Abridged)

Below are the first 15 items. Download the full PDF for the complete list plus editable Excel sheet you can reuse for every plot.

  1. Verify Survey Plan Number: Search Lagos State Surveyor-General’s portal or equivalent in your state. Fake plans reuse old numbers.
  2. Cross-Check Plan vs GIS Coordinates: Use your phone GPS (±5 m) to confirm the beacon positions match the plan.
  3. Government Acquisition Status: Print colour map from Ministry of Physical Planning. Red = acquisition, green = excised, yellow = pending.
  4. Family Gazette (for family land): Obtain certified true copy. Ensure the exact plot number is listed.
  5. Land Use Charge Receipt: Proof that annual tax is paid. Outstanding tax becomes your debt after purchase.
  6. Certificate of Occupancy (C of O): Check expiry date and page number alignment. Photocopies must be coloured.
  7. Deed of Assignment Chain: Trace at least 3 previous transactions. Any missing link = future litigation.
  8. Governor’s Consent: Mandatory for legal transfer. Stamped back page with signature and date.
  9. Survey Beacon Integrity: All 4 beacons must be present and stamped by licensed surveyor. Missing beacon = boundary dispute.
  10. Access Road Legality: Ensure road is on government-approved layout, not a “community path” that can be blocked.
  11. Drainage Setback: Minimum 15 m from major drainage channel. Check Ministry of Environment flood map.
  12. High-Tension Line Setback: 30 m horizontal clearance for 132 kV lines. Get PHCN clearance letter.
  13. Petroleum Pipeline Proximity: Request map from NNPC right-of-way unit. 50 m minimum setback.
  14. Soil Type Visual Inspection: Laterite (red) is good for foundation. Black cotton soil = extra cost on foundation.
  15. Seasonal Flooding History: Visit during peak rain (Sept–Oct). Any stagnant water = walk away.

Common Excuses Sellers Give (And the Truth)

Excuse Reality
“Excision is in process” Means nothing until gazetted. Demand gazette number.
“Family receipt is enough” Not legal for transfer. You need C of O or Governor’s Consent.
“Government will soon approve the road” Politician promise. Insist on existing legal access.
“Survey plan is with the lawyer” Classic delay tactic. Insist on coloured copy before inspection.
“Price goes up tomorrow” Pressure sales. A good plot today will still be good next week.

Tools You Need on Site

  • Smartphone with GPS and Compass apps
  • Measuring wheel or 50 m tape
  • Power bank (GPS drains battery)
  • Printed checklist + pen
  • 1 litre water bottle (pour to check percolation rate)
  • Small shovel (inspect topsoil layer)

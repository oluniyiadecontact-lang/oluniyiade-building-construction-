10 Red Flags When Choosing a Contractor

Real stories, real losses—so you don’t repeat them.

Why This Matters

In 2024, Flying Flourish reviewed 97 contractor disputes totalling ₦1.8 billion in losses. 82% had at least four of the red flags below. Read the stories, memorise the signs.

The 10 Red Flags

1. No Verifiable Previous Site

Story: Mrs Obi paid ₦12 m deposit to a “referred” contractor who showed her photos on his phone. When we asked for an address, he said the estate “doesn’t allow visitors”. We later discovered the photos were lifted from a Kenyan Facebook group.

What to do: Visit at least two previous sites. Speak to the owners in person. If the contractor hesitates, walk away.

2. No Insurance or HSE Policy

Story: A block-maker fell from scaffolding in Ibadan. The client was sued for ₦8 m medical bills because the contractor had no workers’ insurance. Court ruled client was “principal employer”.

What to do: Ask for insurance certificate (public liability + workers’ comp). Verify with the insurer.

3. Prices 30% Below Market

Story: Engineer Ade’s duplex quote was ₦45 m while three others averaged ₦65 m. He awarded the job; contractor disappeared after casting ground floor slab, leaving steel exposed to rain.

What to do: Get three quotes. If one is >20% below, demand line-item BOQ and verify unit rates.

4. No Written Contract

Story: Handshake deal. Midway, contractor increased price by ₦5 m claiming “cement price”. Client had no leg to stand on.

What to do: Insist on a simple contract (JBCC lite). Include price, scope, milestones, retention (5%) and defect period (6 months).

5. Requests Large Upfront Cash

Story: 70% deposit paid (₦18 m). Contractor bought 2 trucks of sand and 1 of blocks, then ghosted. Sand was laterite—not sharp sand—useless for concrete.

What to do: Maximum 30% deposit. Link subsequent payments to milestones certified by an independent agent.

6. No Tax ID or Business Account

Story: Client paid ₦2 m to personal account. Contractor claimed he was “hacked”. Bank said it was a family transfer—no refund.

What to do: Pay only to corporate account matching company name on quote. Ask for TIN; verify on FIRS website.

7. No Safety Gear on Site

Story: Labourer lost eye to flying mortar. NHIS bill ₦1.3 m. Client paid because contractor had no insurance.

What to do: Site inspection: hard hats, boots, gloves, scaffold rails. If missing, safety culture is zero.

8. Constantly Changes Phone Numbers

Story: In 4 months, contractor used 3 different lines. When issues arose, all numbers were “not reachable”.

What to do: Insist on one official line registered on WhatsApp Business. Add to contract.

9. No Evidence of Material Purchase

Story: Contractor claimed he bought 100 bags of cement. Receipts showed 40 bags. Balance was diverted.

What to do: Ask for stamped supplier receipts before releasing each milestone payment.

10. Always in a Hurry to “Start Tomorrow”

Story: Pressure tactic: “If we don’t dig tomorrow, rain will delay us.” Client rushed, skipped soil test. Foundation cracked within 8 months.

What to do: Good contractors are busy. A 1–2 week wait is normal. Use the time to verify docs and drawings.

Bonus: 3-Minute Vetting Script

Copy and paste on WhatsApp:

Hi [Name],
1. Send your company TIN and insurance certificate.
2. Give me two site addresses I can visit this week.
3. Send your last supplier receipt (stamped).
4. Are you available to start in 2 weeks?
5. Accept 30% deposit + milestone payments?
Thanks.

If any item is missing or delayed >24 h, move on.

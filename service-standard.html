Step-by-Step Breakdown

  1. 1. On-boarding & NDA

    Invoice + service agreement + mutual NDA (to protect your drawings) sent. Payment activates slot.

  2. 2. Document Upload

    You provide architectural drawings (even hand-sketch), survey plan, soil test (if available), and any existing quotations.

  3. 3. Session 1 – Strategy & Feasibility (60 min)

    We review drawings for constructability, spot cost-saving alternatives (e.g., swap hollow blocks to sand-crete, adjust roof pitch) and confirm compliance with local building codes.

  4. 4. Permit & Title Review (offline work)

    We verify survey coordinates on state GIS, confirm gazette/excision status and flag any pending acquisition. A 2-page risk report is uploaded to Drive.

  5. 5. BOQ Production (3–4 working days)

    Line-item bill using current market prices (cement ₦5 200/bag, reinforcement ₦830 000/tonne, etc.). Sections:

    • Sub-structure (excavation, footing, ground floor slab)
    • Super-structure (walls, columns, lintels, roof timber)
    • Finishes (floor tiles, painting, ceiling)
    • Services (plumbing, electrical, drainage)
    • External works (gate, fencing, landscaping allowance)
    Each line shows unit, quantity, rate, amount, and 5% contingency.

  6. 6. Vendor Sourcing & Vetting (2–3 days)

    We invite 3 contractors per trade (masonry, roofing, plumbing, electrical, aluminium, tiles). Evaluation matrix covers price, past projects, reference checks, insurance, and HSE compliance. You receive a comparison table plus our recommendation.

  7. 7. Session 2 – Presentation & Scheduling (60 min)

    Screen-share: we walk you through BOQ, vendor quotes and a Gantt chart showing critical path. You choose preferred vendors; we refine the schedule and highlight payment milestones.

  8. 8. Monthly Site Report (optional add-on)

    If your site has commenced, we visit once a month, take progress photos and compare against schedule. Report uploaded to Drive.

  9. 9. 30-Day Support

    Unlimited emails and scheduled calls for any clarification on quotations, material substitutions or payment certificates.

Case Study – Mr & Mrs Okafor (Duplex in Asaba)

Clients living in the UK wanted to start a 4-bedroom duplex in Asaba but feared contractor fraud. They purchased the Standard Package in March 2024.

  • BOQ revealed 12% over-budget compared to their ₦28 million ceiling. We recommended swapping imported roof tiles to stone-coated sheets and switching from suspended timber floor to ground slab, saving ₦3.4 million.
  • Vendor comparison shortlisted a roofing contractor who had done 6 similar jobs in Asaba with reference letters from the UK diaspora.
  • Permit review uncovered that the original survey plan omitted a 15 m drainage setback. We corrected the drawing before submission, avoiding a ₦1.2 million setback penalty.
  • Project started May 2024; first monthly report showed 4% ahead of schedule due to early procurement of steel before price increase.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my own contractor?
Yes. We simply ensure they meet our vetting criteria and sign a fair contract.
What if drawings change after Session 1?
Minor tweaks are covered. Major redesign (>15% floor area) attracts a ₦30 000 revision fee.
Do you handle permit processing?
We prepare and track submissions, but government fees are paid by you directly to the ministry.
How accurate is the BOQ?
±5% variance on material quantities. Market rates valid 30 days; we lock key items with merchants on request.
Is the monthly site visit compulsory?
No. Add only if site has commenced or you want independent progress verification.

Floor Tile Selection Guide 2025

Porcelain vs Ceramic vs Vitrified—lab results + prices + slip ratings.

Quick Answer

  • Living areas: Polished porcelain (600×600 mm) – ₦3.2k/m², slip rating R10
  • Wet areas (bath, kitchen): Matte porcelain R11 – ₦3.5k/m²
  • Low-budget rental: Ceramic 400×400 mm – ₦1.8k/m²
  • High-traffic commercial: Vitrified double-charged – ₦4.8k/m², PEI 4

What the Terms Mean

Water Absorption
<10% = porcelain. Lower = less stain, frost resistant.
Breaking Strength
Higher = can handle heavier loads.
Slip (Pendulum Test)
>0.36 = safe wet, >0.44 = very safe.
PEI Rating
1=wall only, 2=light residential, 3=normal residential, 4=medium commercial, 5=heavy commercial.

Slip Resistance Guide

  • R9 (polished) – dry areas only
  • R10 (normal matte) – living rooms, corridors
  • R11 (coarse matte) – kitchens, balconies
  • R12 (structured) – public wet areas
  • R13 (heavy texture) – industrial

Chipping Test (Drop Ball)

We dropped a 50 g steel ball from 1 m onto each tile (lab-controlled). Results:

  • Ceramic 400×400: chipped at 3rd drop
  • Porcelain 600×600: no chip up to 10 drops
  • Vitrified double-charged: no chip up to 15 drops

Conclusion: use porcelain or vitrified in high-impact areas (kitchen drop zone).

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Buy 1 000+ m² direct from factory depot—save ₦300/m².
  2. Use 600×600 mm instead of 600×1200 mm—cuts laying labour by 25%.
  3. Accept “first choice” grade not “premium”—same tile, 5% cosmetic defect allowed.
  4. Combine living/dining order—one delivery fee.
  5. Buy during Ramadan promo (March–April) – 10% rebate common.

3-Bedroom Bungalow, Abeokuta

₦19 m • 6 months • First-time owner • 18% cost saved

Overview

Mr & Mrs Ogunlana, both civil servants in Abeokuta, wanted a simple 3-bedroom bungalow on their 450 m² family plot. Budget ceiling was ₦20 million. Flying Flourish provided Starter Package guidance plus optional BOQ upgrade.

Key Achievements

  • Total spend ₦19.05 m—₦950 000 under budget
  • Construction duration 6 months (May–Nov 2023)
  • 18% cost saved via bulk material purchase and merchant lock-price
  • Zero variation claims from contractor
  • All utilities (water, septic, solar) functional at hand-over

Cost-Saving Strategies Applied

  1. Bulk cement purchase: 650 bags ordered at ₦4 000/bag vs market ₦5 200 (saved ₦780 000)
  2. Local sand-crete blocks: moulded on site with hired vibrating machine—₦90/block vs ₦140 purchased (saved ₦280 000)
  3. Roofing sheet direct from factory: removed middle-man, saved ₦150 000
  4. Optimized floor plan: reduced corridor space, chopped 8 m² without compromising function—saved ₦400 000 on finishes
  5. Solar bundle deal: 1.5 kW inverter + 4 panels + 2 batteries at trade price (saved ₦120 000)

Challenges & Solutions

Laterite soil with high clay content
Increased footing depth by 200 mm and added 150 mm hardcore before blinding. Cost impact negligible due to on-site sand supply.
Block work labour strike (3 days)
Swift replacement with pre-vetted alternate gang; no net delay because plastering team started on opposite wing.
Tile delivery delay (5 days)
Used buffer days within painting phase; tile team worked Saturday to recover.
Budget creep threat from rising cement price
Early bulk purchase (strategy above) insulated project from mid-year spike.

Kitchen Cost in Nigeria (2025)

From ₦800k cabinet packages to ₦6m luxury setups—real prices, real photos.

Quick Answer

  • Budget package (L-shaped, 3 m base + 2 m wall): ₦800k – ₦1.1 m
  • Mid-range (6 m base + 3 m wall, granite top): ₦2.2 m – ₦2.8 m
  • Luxury (8 m base + 4 m wall, quartz top, built-in appliances): ₦4.5 m – ₦6 m

What the Cost Covers

  1. Cabinets (MDF, HDF, marine ply, acrylic, membrane)
  2. Counter-top (tiles, granite, quartz, solid-surface)
  3. Appliances (hob, hood, oven, microwave, fridge)
  4. Sink & tap
  5. Wall & floor tiles (1.2 m splash-back height)
  6. Electrical (points for appliances, under-cabinet LED)
  7. Plumbing (sink connection, grease trap)
  8. Labour (carpenter, tiler, electrician, plumber)

Appliance Price Snapshot (₦)

  • Table-top 4-burner gas hob: 30k – 45k
  • Built-in 4-burner hob (steel): 65k – 85k
  • 60 cm hood (SS): 80k – 120k
  • 60 cm electric oven: 140k – 220k
  • Microwave (built-in): 150k – 200k
  • Single-door fridge (built-in): 550k – 700k
  • Dishwasher (45 cm): 320k – 450k

Money-Saving Tips

  1. Buy cabinets during Ramadan or December promos—up to 15% discount.
  2. Use local granite; difference vs imported is only colour, not strength.
  3. Combine hob + oven in one unit (combo) saves ₦40k.
  4. Install tiles yourself on Saturday—labour drops ₦600/m².
  5. Choose 600 mm tiles over 300 mm—fewer grout lines, faster install.
